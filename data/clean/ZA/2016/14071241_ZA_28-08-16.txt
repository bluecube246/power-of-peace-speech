Photo : Gianluigi Guercia / AFP  CENTURION -- South Africa posted an imposing 481/8 declared after tea on day two of the second Test against New Zealand in Centurion on Sunday .  After losing the toss and being sent in to bat , each of the top four Proteas batsman moved past 50 . Both Quinton de Kock ( 82 ) and JP Duminy ( 88 ) appeared to be heading for three figures before they were dismissed .  Du Plessis , in particular , was good value for his ton as he upped his strike-rate which lagged around 30 before he moved past 50 , to 47 by the time he decided to declare .  There were meaningful contributions from the majority of the number three Hashim Amla ( 58 ) and Stiaan van Zyl ( 35 ) all helping the South African cause .  South African-born Neil Wagner was the pick of the New Zealand bowlers with a relentless effort on the first two days as he picked up 5/86 .  The declaration , meant the Proteas bowlers would have a menacing 22 overs remaining in the day to try and make inroads into the Kiwi batting line-up .
