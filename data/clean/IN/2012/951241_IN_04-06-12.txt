<h> Public hearing on petro coke plant opposed <p> The Movement for People-Centred Development ( MPCD ) has urged the Government to reconsider its decision on conducting a public hearing on the establishment of a 4.60 lakh tonne capacity calcined petroleum coke plant along with a 25 MW power plant by Sanvira Industries of Hyderabad at Chaatametta in Rambilli mandal of Visakhapatnam district on June 6 . <p> Mr J.V Ratnam , an activist of the organisation , said the local people were strongly opposing the plant which would cause a lot of damage to the environment , and did not want the public hearing to take place as they did not want the industry . <p> He said in a press release that the environment impact assessment ( EIA ) report prepared by the B.S. Envitech of Hyderabad did not contain all relevant information in full . The report said only sulphur dioxide would be released , but in fact the industry would release lead , mercury , and polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons which cause heavy damage to health .
