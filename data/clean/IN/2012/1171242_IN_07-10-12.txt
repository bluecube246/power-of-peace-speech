<p> NEW DELHI : Cautioning aspirants desirous of pursuing educational qualifications through open and distance learning ( ODL ) mode , the Distance Education Council ( DEC ) has asked students to apply only to those programmes approved by it . It has asked the candidates to check the status of distance education provider before applying . <p> This cautionary note , as per a press statement , came in the wake of the council coming across " certain universities/ institutions giving misleading advertisements in the newspapers and through other media for admission in programmes through distance mode indicating that they have approval of the DEC for various programmes . " <p> DEC has advised the aspirants to check the current status of approval/recognition of programmes of universities/ institutions available on the DEC 's website - www.dec.ac.in . <p> " In the absence of DEC approval , qualifications acquired through distance mode shall not be treated recognised for the purpose of employment under the Central Government . " This was announced by the authorities of DEC , a statutory body of the Indira Gandhi National Open University . @ @ @ @ @ @ @ @ @ @ any franchised study centre/ contact point .
