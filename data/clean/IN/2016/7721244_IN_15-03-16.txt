CBSE urged to address grievances of students  The Kerala CBSE School Managements Association has urged the chairman of the Central Board of Secondary Education ( CBSE ) to issue instructions to persons concerned to evolve remedial measures to redress the grievances of Class 12 students , who were in tears after writing the tough Maths examination on Monday .  In a letter sent to CBSE chairman Y.S.K. Seshu Kumar , association president T.P.M. Ibrahim Khan said a large number of students were weeping in the examination hall . The Maths paper was beyond the comprehension and capacity of the students , he said . Mr. Khan pointed out that students were now reluctant to join the senior secondary level in the CBSE stream .  Mass shifting for Class 11  Even last year , there was a mass shifting of students to State and other syllabus for Class 11 .
