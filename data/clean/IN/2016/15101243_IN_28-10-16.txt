<h> ' Mangalmayi Pahat ' infuses divinity into rhythm <p> TNN Oct 29 , 2016 , 03.03 AM IST <p> Nagpur : Melodious songs in reverence to Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj , singing stalwarts KL Sehgal , Mohammed Rafi , sant Sai Baba and other eminent personalities made Shivaji Nagar Park a place of divine rhythm . Over 500 persons had assembled at the park to be part of ' Mangalmayi Pahat ' organized to celebrate Diwali . <p> Singers Aniruddha Joshi , Anandi Joshi , Rasika Joshi and birthday boy Amar Kulkarni presented a blend of Hindi and Marathi songs . They set the tone for morning with the rendition of ' Prabhati sur ' and went on to perform songs like ' Guni baal asa ' , ' Maze maker ' , ' Ketakichya bani ' , ' Khel mandiyela ' , ' Akhiyan sang ' , ' Dipawali manage , ' ' Madhuban main ' and ' Zunjumunju zale ' , among others . <p> " Even though we are representatives of new generation , some things will never fade out -- the charm of old @ @ @ @ @ @ @ @ @ @ closeness with family . Forms may change but the feelings remain , " said Asavari Galande , who anchored the show , weaving together small inspiring anecdotes between the songs . Narrating an incident , she said , " When noted singer KL Sehgal was supposed to present his first song with a sound system at Lahore , there was a technical problem . As there was some commotion , a boy was made to sing . Soon , there was pin-drop silence in the crowd and everyone was enjoying songs rendered by the little boy . This wonder boy is today known as Mohammad Rafi , " said Galande , amid applause from the audience . <p> In an initiative to support Skill India and Make in India , professors of Industrial Training Institute , in collaboration with Bharatiya Janata Yuvak Mandal , successfully produced over 2500 good-quality LED light series . All the components required to produce the lights were sourched from local market and these were the first lights to come with a warranty of one year .
