COCOBOD: Farmers urged to plant high yielding cocoa varieties
http://pulse.com.gh/agriculture/cocobod-farmers-urged-to-plant-high-yielding-cocoa-varieties-id4829726.html

<p> The Executive Director of Cocoa Health Extension Division of the Ghana COCOBOD , Dr Francis Baah , has urged cocoa farmers to plant high yielding cocoa varieties , instead of relying on aged trees with fertilizer . <p> Speaking at the inauguration of the ten-member Cocoa Task Force for the Nkwakwa district , Dr Baah said 60,000 cocoa seedlings will be distributed this year to cocoa farmers . <p> Government has step up measures to boost cocoa production through the distribution of free seedlings to cocoa farmers . <p> However , cocoa farms in mining areas are being destroyed by illegal miners . <p> According to the Chair of the Supreme Consultative Council ( SCC ) of Ghana Cocoa Board ( COCOBOD ) , Alhaji Idriss Alhassan , the cocoa sector could face a major setback if the environmental impacts caused by illegal miners is not addressed . <p> He said large cocoa farms are being destroyed in search for gold , calling on government to stop granting licence to small scale miners operating in cocoa growing areas . <p> Ghana is struggling to meet its targeted @ @ @ @ @ @ @ @ @ @ beans from neighbouring Ivory Coast in the 2014/2015 crop season , according to the finance minister , Seth Terkper . <p> Ecobank predicted cocoa production in Ghana to fall to between 730,000 and 750,000 tonnes following an outbreak of fungal black pod disease last year .
