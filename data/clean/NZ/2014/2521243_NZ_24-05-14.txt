<h> Taxpayers losing out $10m after liquidations <h> Relevant offers <p> Taxpayers are set to lose out on more than $10 million after two property development companies with Manawatu links - including being part-owned by Palmerston North accounting firm partners - were placed into liquidation . <p> Mahia Titan Ltd and Scenic Bay Holdings Ltd , both directed by Palmerston North man Richard Chisholm , were placed into liquidation this month at the request of their shareholders . <p> Reports from liquidator Rod McKenzie show Mahia Titan has $10.3m in debts , while Scenic Bay Holdings owes $3.2m . The only listed creditor for both companies is Crown Asset Management . <p> Crown Asset Management was founded in February 2012 to acquire the assets of five finance companies repaid by the government under the Retail Deposit Guarantee Scheme . <p> Its job is to try to squeeze as much money out of the assets as possible to offset the repayments made under the guarantee scheme . <p> The repayments were triggered by the financial crisis of 2007-2009 , which saw investors lose more than $1b .
