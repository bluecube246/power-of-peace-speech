Pangan : Passing the tax reform package  Pangan : Passing the tax reform package  THEY say death , like taxes , are inevitable . They are sure to come and the impositions may be harsh and drastic , but they would come .  For both , there are justifications for their existence . Taxes are the lifeblood of an economy . Death , as we know it , comes in different forms or manner ; it may be swift , violent or protracted .  President Duterte has at his disposal a P3.35 trillion budget for 2017 which would be funded by this tax reform package .  The four tax reform packages aimed at supporting programs for infrastructure and human capital but at the same time bring down the so-called oppressive tax burden on wage earners will be submitted by the President to Congress . These reform packages include personal income tax , consumption tax , corporate income tax , property tax and capital income tax .  If passed into law , the first package , the personal income tax brackets , their pockets , but at the same time would result in revenue losses amounting to P159 billion .  To offset these losses , the Department of Finance has proposed to expand the value-added tax base by limiting exemptions to only raw food and other necessities like education and health .  The DOF proposes to raise oil excise tax and impose a levy on sugary products and fatty foods .  There are other tax measures proposed to be adopted by the DOF in order to countervail reduction in personal income tax intake . These measures include luxury tax on automobiles , yachts and jewelry , mining taxes , sin taxes on tobacco and alcohol , carbon tax and lottery and casino tax .  ***  Congress in well-advised to scrutinize well the budget proposal of the Duterte administration .  As in the past Aquino administration , there are lump sum items in the budget which have been smartly labeled to cover up sinister motives of congressmen who have insatiable need for huge funds .  More money should be set aside for education and health and the continued and sustained since its beneficiaries are the most marginalized of the society .
