<h> 400 big companies to delay minimum wage <p> Published : 22 June 2013 6:00 PM <p> More than 400 big companies have received the green light to postpone the implementation of minimum wage of RM900 a month until Dec 31 . <p> Human Resource Minister Datuk Richard Riot said the postponement was made due to various reasons , including the financial capability of the companies to implement the minimum wage , announced by Prime Minister Datuk Seri Najib Tun Razak previously . <p> " Overall , all the private sector agreed and did not protest against the decision of the government to implement minimum wage but they asked for flexibility due to certain reasons . <p> " To date , we have received 400 applications from companies seeking for the date to be pushed to Dec 31 and I have approved the applications , " he told reporters after the 2013 Workers ' Day gathering at the Bukit Jalil Stadium today launched by Prime Minister Datuk Seri Najib Tun Razak . <p> The announcement of the RM900 monthly minimum wage for the private sector for the peninsula @ @ @ @ @ @ @ @ @ @ was made by the Prime Minister on April 30 last year and gazetted on July 16 . <p> The Minimum Wage Order 2012 was enforced on Jan 1 , 2013 and the rate would be studied every two years . <p> Meanwhile , Riot said the National Wages Consultative Council would hold a meeting with employers from micro companies to discuss on the implementation of minimum wage on July 25 . <p> He said , to date , the ministry had yet to receive any application for the postponement of minimum wage from micro companies but employers would have an opportunity to do so at the meeting . <p> In his speech , Riot said 2012 witnessed the national economy managing to weather the impact of the global economic crisis .
