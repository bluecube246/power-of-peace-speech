<h> Police warning Dartmouth residents of increase in thefts from vehicles <p> Police are warning residents after a rash of thefts from motor vehicles in the Dartmouth area . <p> Halifax Regional Police has noted an increase in thefts from motor vehicles in the Portland Hills , Portland Estates and Cranberry Crescent areas . <p> Since Oct. 4 , police have received 28 reports of items stolen from unattended vehicles in these areas . In most cases , the vehicles were unlocked with attractive objects left in plain sight . All thefts happened in the middle of the night . <p> " Thefts from vehicles are usually crimes of opportunity , " said Cst . Dianne Woodworth in a news release . " Visible items tempt would-be thieves . " <p> Police say residents should remove all items from their vehicles ( e.g. global positioning systems ( GPS ) , satellite radios , cell phone chargers or other equipment that appears valuable ) and lock the doors upon exiting .
