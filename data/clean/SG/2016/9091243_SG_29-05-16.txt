<h> Family the focus at fourth Car-Free Sunday event <p> A drizzle during the fourth Car-Free Sunday SG event yesterday could not keep picnickers , walkers , cyclists and even pet owners off streets that are usually the domain of vehicles . <p> Luckily for 2,800 families taking part in the Families for Life Walk , the skies cleared in time for the 7.30am flag-off by Minister for Social and Family Development Tan Chuan-Jin . <p> In all , some 13,000 participants enjoyed the 1.5 km walk from the National Gallery Singapore to the Empress Lawn , where they were greeted with music , food and the chance to take part in a fitness workout . <p> The walk is part of efforts to promote resilient families , organised by non-profit organisation Families for Life to coincide with this month 's car-free event . <p> Car-free Sunday SG is a six-month pilot that turns part of the city into a pedestrian- and cyclist-friendly area every last Sunday of the month until July , with each edition featuring a different line-up of activities . <p> At yesterday 's event @ @ @ @ @ @ @ @ @ @ walking trails , or watch a movie outdoors . <p> Mr Tan said having the Families for Life Celebrations together with Car-Free Sunday SG was a good idea , and his ministry would explore doing this more often in future . <p> " There is the novelty of getting to walk around in an area where you do n't normally ... I think it 's a good opportunity for families to get out , and bring the children out together , " he told reporters at the event . <p> Senior staff nurse Suzanne Mak , 31 , who was at the event with nine other members of her immediate and extended family , believes it is a good way to maintain and strengthen familial ties . <p> Mrs Mak said : " At the end of the day , family will always have your back .
